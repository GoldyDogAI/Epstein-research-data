1. CRITICAL: HOW EFTA NUMBERING WORKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EFTA numbers are assigned PER PAGE, not per document. A 10-page PDF starting
at EFTA00000001 consumes EFTA numbers 00000001 through 00000010. The next
document starts at EFTA00000011.

This means:
  • EFTA range (00000001 to 02731783) = 2,731,783 PAGE-numbers
  • Our 1,380,941 documents contain 2,731,825 pages = 100.0% of the range
  • The 42 extra pages are from native spreadsheet ingestion (extra sheets)
  • Verified: 99.997% of consecutive doc pairs satisfy EFTA# + pages = next EFTA#

There are NO "withheld" or "missing" EFTA numbers. Every EFTA page-number in
the range is accounted for by our documents and their page counts.

7. THE "3.5 MILLION PAGES" CLAIM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DOJ claimed "approximately 3.5 million pages" of Epstein files.

What we have:
  EFTA pages:     2,731,825
  Non-EFTA pages:    38,329
  TOTAL:          2,770,154 pages

Gap to claim:       729,846 pages (20.9% shortfall)

This gap CANNOT be explained by:
  • Withheld EFTA documents — 100% of EFTA page-numbers are accounted for
  • Extraction errors — 99.95% of pages have extractable text
  • Database corruption — all 12 datasets fully processed and verified

Possible explanations for the 730K page gap:
  1. DOJ counted pages that were never assigned EFTA numbers (internal/classified)
  2. DOJ included duplicate copies or working drafts in their count
  3. DOJ included materials outside the EFTA system (grand jury exhibits, etc.)
  4. DOJ rounded up significantly from ~2.8M to "approximately 3.5M"
  5. DOJ counted both sides of double-sided scans as separate pages
  6. The count included materials subsequently removed before public release
  7. Additional non-EFTA collections exist that haven't been released

The key finding: Within the EFTA numbering system, coverage is COMPLETE. The
"3.5M" claim refers to something beyond what was released under EFTA numbers.
