PREVIOUS ANALYSIS (INCORRECT):
  The original analysis compared document counts to EFTA range sizes and
  concluded that 1,350,607 documents (49.44%) were "withheld." This was wrong.
  EFTA numbers are assigned PER PAGE, not per document.

CORRECTED FINDING:
  100% of EFTA page-numbers are accounted for. No documents are withheld
  within the EFTA numbering system.

HOW EFTA NUMBERING WORKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EFTA numbers are CONSECUTIVE PAGE IDENTIFIERS:
  • Each page of each document gets a unique sequential EFTA number
  • A 10-page document starting at EFTA00000001 → EFTA00000001 through 00000010
  • The next document starts at EFTA00000011
  • The document's EFTA number (used as filename) is its FIRST page's number

VERIFICATION (across all 1,380,941 documents):
  • 1,380,896 of 1,380,935 consecutive pairs (99.997%) satisfy:
    EFTA_number + page_count = next_document's_EFTA_number
  • The 39 "mismatches" are all NEGATIVE gaps (-36 total) caused by native
    spreadsheet ingestion adding extra sheet pages to existing documents
  • Total EFTA range: 00000001 to 02731783 = 2,731,783 page-numbers
  • Total pages in DB: 2,731,825 (42 more than range, from extra sheets)
  • Coverage: 100.0%

WHY THE ORIGINAL ANALYSIS WAS WRONG
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The original analysis treated EFTA numbers as document identifiers:
  "Expected 2,731,783 documents, found 1,380,941 → 49.44% withheld"

The correct comparison is pages to page-numbers:
  "Expected 2,731,783 pages, found 2,731,825 → 100.0% coverage"

The confusion arose because:
  • 1,380,941 documents contain 2,731,825 pages (avg 1.98 pages/doc)
  • Each document has ONE filename (its first page's EFTA number)
  • But each document CONSUMES multiple EFTA numbers (one per page)
  • So 1.38M documents × ~2 pages/doc = ~2.73M EFTA numbers used

THE REAL QUESTION: WHERE ARE THE OTHER 730K PAGES?

DOJ claimed "approximately 3.5 million pages." We have 2,770,154.

The gap of ~730K pages is NOT explained by withheld EFTA documents (there are
none). Possible explanations:

  1. Pages outside the EFTA system (classified/internal/grand jury materials)
  2. DOJ counting methodology differs (e.g., double-sided pages counted twice)
  3. Duplicate copies or working drafts in DOJ's internal count
  4. Significant rounding ("approximately" doing heavy lifting)
  5. Materials removed between counting and public release
  6. Additional collections that were never EFTA-numbered

This is a legitimate transparency question: what are the ~730K pages the DOJ
counted but didn't assign EFTA numbers to?
